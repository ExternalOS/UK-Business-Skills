Questions

Things people ask before they buy

Five answers, written by a UK sole trader, not a marketing team.

  1. Q1What happens when HMRC rates or statute changes?

    You get the updated file. Free, by email, in the same order line as the original purchase. April rate changes, mid-year case law, new Acas Code wording, all included. The packs are built so the figures live in named cells, not in body text, so an update is a re-export, not a rewrite.

  2. Q2How do I know the documents are actually right?

    Every pack cites the UK legislation and the named case law it rests on in the footnotes, dated to the tax year, so you don't have to take my word for it. Each figure traces back to the gov.uk or HMRC source it came from, and you can check the working yourself before you ever rely on it.

  3. Q3Will these hold up in an inspection or a dispute?

    Each pack is built to the rules it answers to, the MLR 2017 for AML, the Renters' Rights Act 2025 for landlords, the Acas Code for employment, and written to be inspection ready. They're thorough, structured templates, not legal advice, so if you're in a live dispute with real money on it, take the document to a solicitor. For the everyday compliance most independents actually face, it's the file you'd otherwise pay an advisor far more to produce.

  4. Q4What is the refund policy?

    Thirty days, no question. Reply to the receipt with the word "refund" and the original purchase is reversed inside the week. You keep the files; we are not in the business of policing whether you used them in the meantime.

  5. Q5Who are these built for?

    UK independents who handle their own admin. Sole traders, freelancers, micro-employers, private landlords, small letting and estate agents under HMRC AML supervision. If you're running a business on small margins and the SaaS bills are eating the margin, the packs are written for you. If you have a finance team and an accountant already, the packs probably duplicate their work.

Behind the packs

Why this exists

Hi :) I'm James Thom, a sole trader, and I built ExternalOS because I kept needing some of these for my own books, and built the rest because I saw other people were stuck on the same issues. Whenever a compliance question or boring admin came up, the only answers online were a monthly subscription that was priced for a firm with 10+ staff, or an advisor that charged £200/hour for a conversation I didn't really need. Nobody sold the thing I needed itself, the spreadsheet that does the maths, the template with the statute and case law already in it.

So I wrote them. It took about six months to get the five packs out, each one cites the UK legislation and the case law it rests on in the footnotes, so you can check the working yourself. You pay once, and the file is yours. If you find a mistake, or a rate I haven't updated yet, email me and the correction comes back in the same thread as your receipt. 30 day refund, no questions. Free tools on the site too!
